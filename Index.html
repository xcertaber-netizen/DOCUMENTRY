THE DAY WHEN KING OF POP

DIED

Author:TMZ/Date:JUNE 25'2009

morning of June 25, 2009, began deceptively quiet in the grand, rented mansion in Holmby Hills, California, yet beneath the surface, a tragedy was unfolding that would soon seize the world's attention. With his colossal fifty-show "This Is It" comeback series looming in London, Michael Jackson, the incomparable King of Pop, lay asleep, struggling against the crippling insomnia that had plagued him for years. In a desperate and perilous attempt to find rest, Jackson had been administered a dose of the powerful surgical anesthetic, Propofol, by his personal physician, Dr. Conrad Murray. The moment of crisis arrived mid-morning: the doctor returned to find his patient had stopped breathing, his fragile heart succumbing to the drug. The urgent, frantic calls to 911 painted a chilling audio portrait of the unfolding disaster as paramedics rushed to the scene. Though they fought tirelessly, performing CPR first at the bedside and then during the desperate dash to the Ronald Reagan UCLA Medical Center, the icon was pronounced dead at 2:26 PM. Within minutes, the stunning news—a raw alert posted first on a celebrity website—ignited an immediate, global wildfire of disbelief and grief. Across continents, internet traffic surged violently, television programming ceased, and legions of fans poured into city squares, united in an instant, profound, and highly public mourning for the complex, brilliant, and ultimately solitary star whose final, silent exit on that tragic day felt like the abrupt, deafening conclusion of an entire era of pop culture.
